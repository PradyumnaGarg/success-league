Under the present circumstances, the legal ramifications of the current global pandemic are also expected to be considerable. In view of the disruption caused by the COVID-19 pandemic and consequent lockdown announced by the Government, it is quite likely that performances under several contracts might have got delayed, interrupted, or even terminated and cancelled. Parties to such contracts may seek to delay and/or avoid performance of their contractual obligations and/or seek various relief(s) under the ‘force majeure’ clause.

Such non-performance could either be on account of genuine difficulty arising out of COVID 19 or could also be on account of use of the same as an excuse to relieve themselves from an unfavourable bargain. Further, parties may also cite COVID-19 as a basis for renegotiation of price or other key contractual provisions. It will also be relevant to see as to how the Courts are going to consider COVID-19 as a ‘force majeure’ event in the commercial contracts.

Success League is keeping a ‘Keen Eye’ on all the major developments in this regard so as to provide best-in-class emergency assistance to its valuable clients.
